# Economic Moat

> **Definition:** An economic moat is a sustainable competitive advantage that protects a business from competitors, allowing it to maintain profitability over time.

## The Metaphor

Just as medieval castles were protected by moats of water, businesses with economic moats are protected from competitive attacks. The wider and deeper the moat, the more durable the business.

## Types of Moats

### 1. Cost Advantage
Companies that can produce or deliver goods/services at lower cost than competitors.
- Economies of scale
- Access to cheaper inputs or distribution

### 2. Intangible Assets
- Brand names (Coca-Cola, Apple)
- Patents or intellectual property
- Government licenses or franchises

### 3. Switching Costs
When leaving a product or service is expensive or inconvenient for customers.
- Bank accounts
- Software ecosystems
- Healthcare providers

### 4. Network Effects
The product becomes more valuable as more people use it.
- Credit card networks
- Social platforms

## Buffett's View

> "The key to investing is not assessing how much an industry is going to affect society, or how much it will grow, but rather determining the competitive advantage of any given business and, above all, the durability of that advantage."
> — 1995 Annual Letter

> "A truly great business must have an enduring 'moat' that protects excellent returns on invested capital."
> — 2007 Annual Letter
