# Book Value

> **Definition:** Book value is the net asset value of a company - total assets minus intangible assets and liabilities, as recorded on the balance sheet.

## Formula

```
Book Value = Total Assets - Total Liabilities - Intangible Assets
```

Or more simply:
```
Book Value = Shareholders' Equity
```

## Relationship to Intrinsic Value

> "Book value is an accounting concept that records the historical cost of assets. Intrinsic value is an economic concept that estimates the future cash-generating ability of those assets."
> — 1977 Annual Letter

**Key Insight:** A business with a wide gap between intrinsic value and book value likely has intangible assets (moat) that don't appear on the balance sheet.

## Buffett's Approach

In early Berkshire years, Buffett focused on companies trading below book value. Later, he shifted to quality businesses with high returns on equity, even at premium prices.

> "It's far better to buy a wonderful business at a fair price than a fair business at a wonderful price."
> — 1989 Annual Letter
