# Intrinsic Value

> **Definition:** Intrinsic value is the discounted value of all future cash flows that a business will generate. It is the fundamental concept underlying value investing.

## Origin

The concept was formally articulated by John Burr Williams in his 1938 book, "The Theory of Investment Value." Williams argued that the value of a business should be determined by the present value of its future dividend payments and earnings.

Benjamin Graham and David Dodd refined this concept in their 1934 classic "Security Analysis," teaching Buffett the mathematical framework for calculating intrinsic value.

## Buffett's Definition

> "Intrinsic value is the discounted value of all future cash flows that a business will generate. It is not a precise number - but rather a range of values that reasonable estimates of future cash flows, properly discounted, might produce."
> — 1989 Annual Letter

## Key Principles

1. **Future Cash Flows**: A business is worth the cash it will generate over its lifetime
2. **Discount Rate**: Future cash is worth less than present cash - use risk-free rate as benchmark
3. **Business Quality**: Higher quality businesses with predictable cash flows deserve higher multiples

## Why It Matters

> "The difference between a good business and a bad business is that good businesses generate lots of cash; bad businesses consume it."
> — Charlie Munger
